How to make the computer clever:
The idea is to simulate a "would-win" move: the computer scans all columns for potential threats where the human could win with their next move, and prioritizes blocking those threats. If no blocking is needed, it picks a random column.

Am I able to accomplish this:
Yes.
Is the computer smart enough:
No. The computer only focuses on blocking the human player, without trying to win itself.

About the algorithm:
Select a temporary available cell on the game board, and check if this cell can form a 4-circle win for the human player, and undo the move. If the cell can help the human player win, return True, otherwise, return False.

How often the computer beats the human player:
Around 70% of the time

Possible improvement:
Aside from blocking human players, the improvement lies in that the computer should try to win itself, in other words, if no blocking moves are there, it should prioritise a step that helps itself connect 4 circles.
